Proof that queer female-led stories are needed amid numerous cancellations

Published on Oct 20, 2023

The box office success of the recent movie 'Bottoms' is bright light among the onslaught of cancellations of sapphic series.

Netflix is notoriously guilty in this department, only extending at most to the second season if lucky. They have faced backlash for axing Warrior Nun, First kill and numerous other shows this past year.

LGBT representation in media is of paramount importance, as it plays a significant role in fostering inclusivity, promoting understanding, and empowering individuals within the LGBTQ+ community. When such representations are consistently canceled or denied, it not only deprives the community of vital visibility but also perpetuates stereotypes and misconceptions. Canceling LGBTQ+ stories and characters sends a message that their experiences are not valid or worthy of acknowledgment, which can be deeply alienating and detrimental to those seeking relatable role models in the media. Moreover, it inhibits progress toward a more diverse and accepting society where all voices and stories are not only heard but celebrated. Ensuring accurate and positive representation is a vital step in building a more inclusive and equitable world for everyone.

Feminist Analysis of the Barbie movie

Published on Sept 15, 2023

From a feminist perspective, the new Barbie movie holds the potential to be a powerful tool for advocating gender equality and empowerment. Historically, Barbie has been a subject of controversy in feminist discussions due to her portrayal reinforcing unrealistic beauty standards and traditional gender roles. However, the recent evolution of Barbie as a symbol of diverse capabilities and aspirations for women has been commendable. This movie seems to encapsulate this shift, portraying Barbie not as a mere fashion icon but as a multifaceted character capable of achieving any role or profession, irrespective of gender norms. The film's focus on encouraging girls to aspire to diverse careers, from being a president to an astronaut or a chef, aligns with feminist values by promoting the idea that women can excel in any field they choose. By showcasing Barbie's versatility and emphasizing that a woman's potential isn't limited by societal expectations, this movie aligns with feminist ideals of gender equality, empowerment, and breaking traditional stereotypes.
